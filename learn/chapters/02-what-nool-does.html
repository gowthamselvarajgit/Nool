Chapter 02 · The Big Picture

What NOOL Does —
The three roles & daily workflow

Before any code, you need to know who uses NOOL and what they do with it every day. Knowing this makes every code file we read later make sense.

🎯 What NOOL Does in One Paragraph

NOOL is a complete ERP system (Enterprise Resource Planning — a fancy name for "a tool that runs your whole business") for a saree-polishing workshop. It tracks every saree that enters and leaves the workshop, records every worker's daily polishing, calculates everyone's salary, tracks every rupee paid by or to owners, and gives my parents a real-time picture of how the business is doing — all from a phone or laptop browser.

👥 The Three Kinds of People Who Log In

Every person who can log into NOOL falls into one of three categories. In the code, this is enforced by a Java enum (a fixed list of allowed values) called Role with exactly three options: ADMIN, WORKER, and SAREE_OWNER. Each role sees a completely different version of the app.

Line-by-line — what each role can do

1 Role.ADMIN — my mom & dad
They run the workshop. They can add & remove workers, add & remove owners, mark attendance, record sarees received and returned, pay salaries, collect payments, and view every dashboard. Admins can see everyone's data.
2 Role.WORKER — Prema, Ravi, and other polishers
They polish sarees. They can see only their own attendance, daily polish counts, salary earned, and salary received. They cannot view other workers' data, cannot add new employees, and cannot see owner-side data.
3 Role.SAREE_OWNER — Rahul and other clients
They bring sarees to be polished. They see only their own inventory (how many of their sarees are still with the workshop), their receive/return history, and what they've paid vs still owe. They cannot see other owners' data, cannot see workers, and cannot modify anything.
👑

ADMIN

🎮 Play the game — role-based access in action

😰 Prema tries to view ALL workers
Action 
Prema (WORKER) hits:
  POST /api/employees/list

(an admin-only endpoint
to see every worker)
→
Response 
HTTP 403 Forbidden

{
  "status": 403,
  "error": "Forbidden",
  "message": "Access Denied"
}
The backend reads Prema's JWT (her login ID-card), sees role = WORKER, and the security rules say "only ADMIN can call /employees/list". Rejected with no data leak. The protection lives in both the frontend (the button doesn't appear in Prema's UI) and the backend (even if she crafts the request manually, it's blocked).
🎮 Prema views HER OWN attendance
Action 
Prema (WORKER) hits:
  POST /api/attendance/my-list

(a worker-friendly endpoint
that returns "my own" data)
→
Response 
HTTP 200 OK

[
  { "date": "2026-05-15",
    "status": "PRESENT" },
  { "date": "2026-05-14",
    "status": "PRESENT" },
  { "date": "2026-05-13",
    "status": "ABSENT" }
]
The backend reads Prema's employee ID from her JWT and returns ONLY her rows. She never has to send "give me employee 5's data" — the server already knows who she is and serves her own data automatically.
The pattern: Endpoints with /my-* in the URL automatically filter to "your own" data. Endpoints without it (like /list) are admin-only and show everyone's data. We'll see this convention applied across every module — Attendance, Daily Work, Salary, Inventory, Payments.

🎬 A Saree's Journey — The Whole Cycle

Let's follow ONE saree from the moment Rahul drops it off until the moment everyone gets paid. This is the central workflow of NOOL.

🧵
1. Receive

Owner brings sarees. Admin records a RECEIPT in NOOL.

📋
2. Hold

Workshop "has" the sarees. Inventory shows them as in-hand.

✨
3. Polish

Worker polishes. Admin logs daily counts (fresh + re-polish).

📦
4. Return

Sarees go back to owner. Admin records a RETURN.

💰
5. Pay

Owner pays the workshop. Workshop pays its workers.

Line-by-line through one real week

Mon Rahul drops off 100 sarees
Rahul arrives at the workshop on Monday May 11. He hands over a bundle of 100 sarees. Dad opens NOOL → Inventory → Add Receipt → picks "Rahul Textiles", types quantity 100, date today, remarks "Diwali batch". One row is now in the database marked RECEIPT. Rahul's balance with us: 100 sarees in hand.
Tue Prema polishes 10 sarees
Tuesday morning Prema arrives. Mom marks her PRESENT. At end of day Dad asks "how many today?" — she says 8 fresh + 2 re-polish. Dad opens NOOL → Daily Work → Add → picks Prema, types 8 and 2. Her earnings for the day: (8 + 2) × ₹30 = ₹300.
Wed Same routine continues
Wednesday: Prema does 9 fresh + 1 re-polish = ₹300 again. Mom marks her PRESENT. Ravi (another worker) does 6 fresh + 3 re-polish at his rate of ₹25 = ₹225 earned.
Thu Prema is absent (sick day)
Mom marks her ABSENT for Thursday. No daily work row is recorded for her that day — she didn't earn anything. Other workers continue.
Fri Dad returns 50 polished sarees to Rahul
Friday afternoon, Dad delivers 50 finished sarees back to Rahul. Records a RETURN in NOOL. Rahul's balance is now 100 − 50 = 50 still being polished. Rahul now owes: 50 × ₹65 = ₹3,250. He sends ₹3,000 by UPI; Dad records the OwnerPayment. Rahul's pending: ₹250.
Sat Pay-day for Prema
Saturday Dad sums up the week's daily-work rows for Prema: 4 working days × ~10 sarees × ₹30 = ₹1,200 earned. He pays her ₹1,000 in cash and records the SalaryPayment. Her pending salary: ₹200.

💵 How Money Flows — Two Separate Streams

Money in the workshop moves in two completely independent streams. It's important to keep them separate in your head — they live in different database tables and follow different formulas.

Stream 1 — Money coming IN (from owners)

Each owner has their own rate. When sarees are returned, they owe:

Owner Payable
totalPayable = sareesReturned × ratePerSaree
🎮 Compute what Rahul owes this month
Inputs 
sareesReturned = 80
rahul.ratePerSaree = ₹65
amountAlreadyPaid = ₹4,000
→
NOOL computes 
totalPayable
  = 80 × ₹65
  = ₹5,200

pendingAmount
  = ₹5,200 − ₹4,000
  = ₹1,200 owed
These three numbers come from three different tables: sareesReturned from saree_ledger, ratePerSaree from saree_owner, and amountAlreadyPaid from owner_payment. The dashboard joins them together to show one tidy "Pending: ₹1,200".

Stream 2 — Money going OUT (to workers)

Each worker has their own polish rate. Each day they earn:

Worker Earnings (per day)
earnings = (freshCount + rePolishCount) × polishingRate
🎮 Compute Prema's salary for a week
Inputs (5 days) 
Mon: 8 fresh + 2 repolish
Tue: 9 fresh + 1 repolish
Wed: 7 fresh + 3 repolish
Thu: absent (no row)
Fri: 10 fresh + 0 repolish
prema.polishingRate = ₹30
alreadyPaid = ₹1,000
→
NOOL computes 
Mon:  10 × 30 = 300
Tue:  10 × 30 = 300
Wed:  10 × 30 = 300
Thu:   0
Fri:  10 × 30 = 300

Total earned: ₹1,200
Already paid: ₹1,000
Pending:        ₹200
Each day's number is computed on the fly using Prema's current rate. NOOL recomputes with the current rate every time it's queried (see Chapter 36 for the trade-off discussion).
The workshop's profit per saree = owner's rate (₹65) − worker's rate (₹30) = ₹35. That's how my parents' workshop stays in business. NOOL doesn't compute this number directly, but you can see both streams side by side in the admin dashboard.

🚫 What NOOL Does NOT Do (Honest Disclosure)

To set expectations, here are things NOOL doesn't do — either because they weren't needed, or I left them for future versions:

1 No GST or tax calculations
My parents' workshop is below the GST threshold in India. If needed later, this could be added as a separate computation on top of owner payments.
2 No SMS or email notifications
All communication still happens by phone call. Adding SMS would mean integrating an SMS gateway like Twilio or MSG91 — beyond NOOL's current scope.
3 No multi-workshop support
NOOL is single-tenant — one workshop per installation. If you wanted to run NOOL for multiple workshops sharing one database, you'd need to add a "workshop_id" column to most tables.
4 No mobile app
NOOL is a responsive web app — it works fine on phone browsers. A native Android/iOS app could be added later, but the web version is enough today.
5 No automatic backups
The database is backed up manually for now. Adding a scheduled mysqldump cron job is a one-day task someone could pick up.
6 No PDF invoice generation
Currently invoices are verbal — Dad tells Rahul "you owe ₹3,250" over the phone. Adding PDF generation (e.g., using OpenPDF or iText) is a possible future enhancement.

These are deliberate trade-offs. NOOL is built for one workshop's actual needs, not hypothetical ones. The principle: don't build features nobody asked for.
