Chapter 02 · The Big Picture

What NOOL Does —
The three roles & daily workflow

Before we dive into code, let's understand who uses NOOL and what they do with it every day. Knowing this will make every code file we read later make much more sense.

🎯 What NOOL Does in One Paragraph

NOOL is a complete Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system for a saree-polishing workshop. It tracks every saree that enters and leaves the workshop, records every worker's daily polishing, calculates everyone's salary, tracks every rupee paid by or to owners, and gives my parents a real-time picture of how the business is doing — all from a phone or laptop browser.

👥 The Three Kinds of People Who Log In

Every person who can log into NOOL falls into one of three categories. In the code, this is enforced by a Java enum called Role with exactly three values: ADMIN, WORKER, and SAREE_OWNER. Each role sees a completely different version of the app.

👑

ADMIN

That's my mom & dad. They run the whole business.

  • Add & manage workers
  • Add & manage owners (clients)
  • Record sarees received from owners
  • Record sarees returned to owners
  • Mark daily attendance
  • Log how many sarees each worker polished
  • Pay salaries to workers
  • Record payments coming in from owners
  • See business-wide dashboard & analytics
  • Export everything to Excel
👷

WORKER

The polishers — like Prema, Ravi, and the others.

  • See own dashboard with today's stats
  • View own profile
  • See own attendance calendar
  • Log own daily work (fresh + re-polish counts)
  • See own salary history
  • See own earnings vs. amount paid
  • See own performance trends
🧵

SAREE_OWNER

The clients — like Rahul, Sharma Madam, etc.

  • See own dashboard
  • View own profile
  • See how many sarees still with the workshop
  • View full receive/return history (their ledger)
  • See how much they've paid vs. owe
  • See their payment history
🔐 The protection rule: Each role can ONLY see their own stuff. A worker cannot see another worker's salary. An owner cannot see another owner's sarees. This is enforced both in the frontend (the wrong buttons don't even appear) AND in the backend (so even a clever attacker can't bypass it). We'll cover this in detail when we get to security chapters (Chapters 20-23).

🎬 A Saree's Journey — The Whole Cycle

Let's follow ONE saree from the moment Rahul drops it off until the moment everyone gets paid. This is the central workflow of NOOL — every chapter from now on will reference some step of this journey.

🧵
1. Receive

Owner brings sarees. Admin records a RECEIPT in NOOL.

📋
2. Hold

Workshop now "has" the sarees. Inventory shows them as in-hand.

✨
3. Polish

Worker polishes. Admin logs daily counts (fresh + re-polish).

📦
4. Return

Sarees go back to owner. Admin records a RETURN.

💰
5. Pay

Owner pays the workshop. Workshop pays its workers.

📅 A Real Week at the Workshop

Let's walk through one week using our cast. Dates use the year 2026 for clarity.

Monday, May 11, 2026 — Rahul drops off 100 sarees
  • Rahul arrives with a big bundle. Tells Dad: "100 sarees, Diwali batch."
  • Dad opens NOOL → Inventory → Add Receipt
  • Picks "Rahul Textiles", types 100, date 2026-05-11, remarks "Diwali batch"
  • In the database: a new row is inserted into the saree_ledger table with entry_type = RECEIPT, quantity = 100.
  • Rahul's "in-hand" balance jumps to 100.
Tuesday-Wednesday — Prema & the team polish
  • Tuesday: Prema polishes 8 fresh + 2 re-polish = 10 sarees
  • Dad opens NOOL → Daily Work → Add. Picks Prema, types 8 and 2, date today.
  • In the database: a new row in employee_daily_work table.
  • Prema's earnings for the day = (8 + 2) × ₹30 = ₹300
  • Wednesday: similar entry, similar maths.
Thursday, May 14 — Mom marks attendance
  • Every morning Mom opens NOOL → Attendance and ticks who's here.
  • Prema: PRESENT. Ravi: PRESENT. (Another worker took the day off — Mom marks LEAVE.)
  • In the database: three rows in the attendance table for today.
  • Each worker's attendance calendar updates instantly.
Friday — Dad returns 50 polished sarees to Rahul
  • Dad opens NOOL → Inventory → Add Return. Rahul Textiles, 50 sarees.
  • Important: NOOL first checks "how many do we still have for Rahul?" Computes: 100 received − 0 returned = 100 in hand. 50 ≤ 100? YES. Approved.
  • Inserts a new ledger row with entry_type = RETURN, quantity = 50.
  • Rahul's new in-hand balance: 100 − 50 = 50 still with us.
Friday evening — Owner payment & worker salary
  • Rahul owes: 50 returned × ₹65 = ₹3,250
  • He sends ₹3,000 via UPI. Dad records it: Owner Payments → Add.
  • His pending amount: ₹3,250 − ₹3,000 = ₹250 still pending.
  • Same evening, Mom checks Prema's earnings this week — say ₹1,500 — and decides to pay her ₹1,000 as advance.
  • Salary → Pay. Prema, ₹1,000, today.
  • Prema's pending salary: ₹1,500 − ₹1,000 = ₹500.

That's a typical week. Every single transaction is logged. Every balance is recomputed instantly. No paper notebook required. This is what NOOL does.

💵 How Money Flows — Two Separate Streams

There are two completely independent money flows in NOOL. Important to keep them separate:

Stream 1 — Money coming IN (from owners)

Each owner has their own rate. When sarees are returned, they owe:

Owner Payable
totalPayable = sareesReturned × ratePerSaree

Stream 2 — Money going OUT (to workers)

Each worker has their own polish rate. Each day they earn:

Worker Earnings (per day)
earnings = (freshCount + rePolishCount) × polishingRate
The workshop's profit per saree = owner's rate (₹65) − worker's rate (₹30) = ₹35. That's how my parents' workshop stays in business. NOOL doesn't compute this number directly — but you can see both streams side by side in the analytics dashboard.

🚫 What NOOL Does NOT Do (Honest Disclosure)

To set expectations, here are things NOOL does not do — either because they weren't needed, or because I left them for future versions:

  • No GST/tax calculations. My parents' workshop is below the GST threshold. If needed, this can be added later.
  • No SMS or email notifications. All communication still happens by phone call.
  • No multi-workshop / multi-tenancy. NOOL is currently single-tenant.
  • No mobile app. It's a responsive web app — works fine on phone browsers.
  • No automatic backups. The DB is backed up manually (something to improve).
  • No PDF invoice generation. Could be added — currently invoices are verbal.

These are deliberate trade-offs. NOOL is built for one workshop's actual needs, not hypothetical ones. The principle: don't build features nobody asked for.
