Chapter 01 · Where We Begin

The Real Story —
Why NOOL exists ❤️

Before we touch any code, you need to know why this project was built. Because it wasn't built for a class, or a portfolio, or a paycheck. It was built for two people — my mom and dad — to make their saree-polishing workshop easier to run.

👨‍👩‍👦
This isn't a hypothetical project.
It's a love letter from a son to his parents.

My mom and dad have been running a small saree-polishing workshop in India for many, many years. Every day, owners bring big batches of sarees to be polished. My parents take them in, get them polished by their workers, and return the finished sarees back to the owners. The owners pay them based on how many sarees came back polished, and my parents in turn pay their workers based on how many sarees each one polished.

For all these years, I watched them write everything in paper notebooks. One notebook for owners. One for workers. One for daily attendance. One for payments. And every single evening I would see the same scene play out — pages flipped back and forth, eyes squinting at faded entries, my mom asking my dad, "How many sarees of Rahul are still with us?", my dad asking my mom, "Did we pay Prema for last Friday or not?" Numbers crossed out and rewritten. Confusion. Tiredness.

I couldn't watch them struggle like that anymore. So I built NOOL — a complete digital system that does all the accounting, all the tracking, all the calculations for them. No more notebooks. No more confusion. Just one clean app that remembers every single saree, every rupee, every working day.

Everything you're about to learn was built so my parents can finally rest easy in the evenings. ❤️

🧵 What the Workshop Actually Does — in 4 Steps

Before we go further, let's understand the business itself. It's a simple cycle that repeats every day. Here it is as a "play the game" walk-through — give the workshop some sarees as input, watch what comes out.

🎮 The basic workshop cycle
Input — Monday 
Rahul (saree owner) brings:
  100 raw sarees
  Rate agreed: ₹65 per saree
→
Output — by Friday 
Workshop returns:
  100 polished sarees
  Rahul pays: 100 × ₹65 = ₹6,500
  Workers paid based on
  who polished how many
That's the entire business in one cycle. Sarees in, polished sarees out, money flows in both directions. NOOL's job is to track every saree and every rupee through this cycle so my parents always know the current state.

The 4 steps in detail

1 Owner brings sarees
A saree owner — say Rahul, who runs a clothing shop — drops off a batch of unpolished sarees at the workshop. He and my dad have already agreed on a price per saree (₹65 in his case). My dad notes this down in NOOL as a RECEIPT (sarees received).
2 Workers polish them
Each worker polishes some sarees that day. My dad notes who polished how many. For example: "Prema polished 8 fresh + 2 re-polish sarees today." Each worker has their own per-saree pay rate — that determines their daily earnings.
3 Sarees go back to owner
Once enough sarees are ready, my dad delivers them back to Rahul. This is recorded in NOOL as a RETURN. Now NOOL knows how many of Rahul's sarees are still with the workshop (received minus returned).
4 Money flows
Rahul pays my parents based on how many sarees came back. My parents pay their workers based on each worker's daily output. NOOL tracks both flows: "Rahul still owes ₹500", "Prema is still owed ₹1,200 in salary".

👥 The Three Kinds of People in NOOL

Through the rest of this tutorial, you'll keep seeing three names: Mom & Dad, Rahul, and Prema. They aren't random — they each represent one of the three kinds of users in NOOL.

👑

ADMIN — Mom & Dad

They own the workshop. They see and can change everything.

  • Add or remove workers and owners
  • Record sarees received and returned
  • Mark daily attendance
  • Log how many sarees each worker did
  • Pay salaries; record customer payments
👷

WORKER — Prema

The polisher. Sees only her own data.

  • Sees her own attendance for the month
  • Sees her own daily polish counts
  • Sees her own earnings & salary paid
  • Cannot see other workers' data
🧵

SAREE_OWNER — Rahul

The client. Sees only his own sarees.

  • Sees how many of his sarees are with us
  • Sees his receive/return history
  • Sees what he's paid & what he still owes
  • Cannot see other owners' data

📓 The Pain NOOL Fixed

Before NOOL, my parents managed everything in paper notebooks. Let me show you what a typical question looked like — and how slow the answer was.

😰 Before NOOL — paper notebook game
Question 
"How many of Rahul's
sarees are still with us?"
→
What happens 
1. Find Rahul's page
   in receipt notebook
2. Find Rahul's pages
   in return notebook
3. Add up receipts
4. Add up returns
5. Subtract by hand
~ 20 minutes
Often wrong!
Every simple question took 15-20 minutes of flipping pages. And after a long day, the math wouldn't always be right. This is the problem NOOL was built to solve.
😊 After NOOL — same question, in seconds
Action 
Mom opens NOOL → clicks
on Rahul's row in the
inventory list.
→
Result (instant) 
Rahul Textiles
  Received: 100
  Returned: 70
  In hand:  30 sarees
Generated in < 1 second.
Same question, almost zero effort. The numbers come from one formula NOOL runs for you: inHand = sum(receipts) − sum(returns). We'll see exactly where this lives in the code in Chapter 15.

What NOOL replaces

1 Paper "received" notebook
Every time Rahul or another owner dropped off sarees, Mom or Dad wrote "Rahul 100 — Monday" in a notebook. NOOL replaces this with a single form in the Inventory page — one click, one row saved forever.
2 Paper "returned" notebook
Same thing for sarees going back to owners. Another notebook, another column. NOOL handles this in the same Inventory page — a "Return" form parallel to the "Receipt" form.
3 Worker daily diary
Each worker had a page where Dad noted how many sarees they polished each day. NOOL replaces this with the Daily Work module — one row per (worker, date), with fresh and re-polish counts.
4 Attendance register
A separate notebook with ticks and crosses for who came in each day. NOOL replaces this with the Attendance module — and shows it as a colored monthly calendar so patterns are easy to spot.
5 Salary book
Yet another notebook with every payment to every worker. NOOL replaces this with the Salary module, which also computes earnings automatically from the daily work rows — no manual math.
6 Customer payment book
The book where incoming money was tracked. NOOL replaces this with the Owner Payments module, which computes "what you still owe us" automatically from the return ledger and the per-owner rate.
